1. Reports

  • Secretary’s update on digital conversion:

    We have anew free site at Github, a $1 a month email address, and the help from a professional programmer; and will soon transfer our domain name to Github. The aim is to set up an easy automatic system — progress is slow.

  • New Local Government Act raised by Pauline Tonkin:

    Bev Dorman replied that she is aware that a new Local Government review is underway. A Shire report had been sent to the Local Government Advisory Board (LGAB).

    Bev has sent a lengthy document criticising the Shire’s position; noting that they had ignored the views raised by the community. The Shire had to get their report in by Feb 14th, she had written a lengthy response. Pauline asked if the community views were not included, what course of action might be taken.

    Bev replied that she would lodge a complaint with the board, she had added a note to the first document that included a motion made at the meeting on the issue, and the fact that the Shire had substituted their own views for those things actually raised by the community.

    The board is drawn from former mayors and associated parties, she opined that she was unsure how fairly complaints would be entertained by the Board. Pauline had sent a document about the biased use of statistics, where favourable responses are recorded, but unfavourable ones are dismissed as not representing the community.

    Pauline, who has been going through the associations records, has found many such instance, one she recalled when a ward that represented 40% of the Shire was allowed one representative within the Shire council. At the time 750 people responded, but the council ignored their wishes. A more recent example was when they were seeking City status, residents who opposed it were dismissed as too old and ‘could not cope with change’; dismissed what they had proposed as being statically unrepresented.

    The Bill has not been tabled, or circulated as a draft, a report has been circulated. There appears to be no monitoring process of breaches of the Act, which appears to be very frequent across Perth, and is expensive to investigate.

    There is an election in October. A desire for a single electorate was raised.

2. Correspondence

Pauline Tonkin, emailed the following; as its contents are of general interest to the KDRRA, I have made a copy; an addendum to Pauline’s report.

Further to our discussion Thursday night re Local Govt Acts etc.

In 2007 I did an Analysis of May 2007 Report of Standing Committee on Environment & Public Affairs on Local Govt. Amendment Bill (no.2) 2006.

 116 submissions received (78 against & 35 supported).

Most of those 78 submissions against the change to the current voting system were from Local Govt. Authorities and therefore appear to have been summarily dismissed, as were my own submission, the Lesmurdie Ratepayers Association and Kalamunda Shire’s contribution.

However, the 35 supporting submissions must have been given more credence – especially the hitherto unknown lobby group called “The Proportional Representation Society of Australia”.

  1. There was a complete lack of detailed reasons or demand for a change to the voting system from ratepayers.

  2. There was no public benefit or improved democratic voting outcomes demonstrated or identified.

The last part of the email includes Pauline’s opinion, which is valuable, but will only be included with her permission.

2. Dissolution of the KDRRA?

discussion:
Peter Forrest refers to the constitution of the KDRRA and objectives discontinued if it is dissolved and if continued has to be adjusted to meeting those objectives.

Pauline Tonkin replies that the only question is whether we dissolve or not.

Bev Dornan stated that the other alternative is to simply freeze the organisation for a year and revisit the issue. Several voices support her proposition.

Greg Schofield, replies that due to the Covid crisis, we have to have the AGMs as a legal requirement, we can decide not to have any meeting at this AGM until July 2022. No money is to be spent, everyone remains responsible for their areas of responsibility, unless some urgent natter arises, then the committee can call a meeting (the elected officials from this meeting, plus a committed member).

Pauline raises the question of liabilities?

Greg replies that as is a voluntary organisation we have no statutory responsibilities, tax or other financial liabilities.

Peter states that we are bound by the incorporation guides.

Greg replies that under law if we have a good reason; under Covid restrictions that decisions made in one meeting are not likely to be fulfilled by the next meeting. An opinion confirmed by several at the meeting that Covid covers such a decision.

Nat as sitting Chair rules that Covid has given us a lot of leeway.

Greg as acting secretary mentions there are nomination for those positions that can be elected at this meeting, if the organisation is not dissolved. Pat states the we need to have elections.

Greg thanks Andrea Balfe for her voluntary work as a treasurer, which may be an exception to transfer the account, Andrea suggests as nothing will be done in the period she is prepared to remain in-situ.

The motion to dissolve falls if it is not moved or seconded, the sitting Chair only needs to put this new motion on the table and it supersedes the other also.

Note:

Ref., “Business arising from previous minutes”

The actual wording of the No Confidence motion at the meeting was a motion of ‘No Confidence in the Chair’ in a particular meeting. A vote of no confidence in the Chairperson is a motion when the organisation feels the Chairperson, an elected position, no longer has the confidence of the majority members of the organisation. Such a motion requires prior notice be given to all members — if the motion is carried the Chairperson must resign from the position, or is disposed. In both cases if the sitting chair is the object of the motion, they must step down; the Vice-Chairperson then presides over the vote, or some other person nominated by the meeting, there is no debate of the motion as it is a procedural matter.
